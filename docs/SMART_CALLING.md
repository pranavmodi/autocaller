# Smart Calling (Art Sobczak) — Distilled for the Autocaller

Source: *Smart Calling: Eliminate the Fear, Failure, and Rejection from Cold Calling* by Art Sobczak. Distilled for the attorney BD autocaller (Twilio + OpenAI Realtime + Gemini voice, calling personal-injury lawyers to book Possible Minds demos).

---

## 1. Smart vs Dumb — pre-call intelligence
A "dumb" cold call dials blind and recites the same pitch. A **Smart Call** uses intel before dialing + a conversational, consultative opener. Required intel: firm facts (practice area, size, locations), **trigger events** (bad experience, change/transition, new awareness), and personal signals (name pronunciation, recent posts/articles).

**LLM rule:** never open without one tailored data point. If none exists, lead with an industry-specific signal — do not fabricate personalization.

**Social engineering (talk to anyone-not-the-buyer):** 4 steps — (1) identify self+co, (2) "I hope you can help me out," (3) justification clause ("so I'm better prepared when I talk to…"), (4) ask.

## 2. Possible Value Proposition (PVP)
The *end-result value* in the buyer's language, not the product. Always "possible" — value is perceived.

Rules:
- Name the outcome, not the feature ("tech support answered first call, no hold" — not "great support").
- Combine pain + gain: "We work with landlords whose properties failed inspection and fix them so they pass."
- **Weasel words** mandatory on first call: *might, maybe, perhaps, depending on*. Declarative claims trigger defensiveness.
- Anchor in prior-customer results with **precise numbers**: "274 installations" beats "hundreds."

**Banned PVP phrases:** save you time and money, cost-effective, commitment to quality, largest/most experienced, been in business X years, "free."

**For PI lawyers:** frame around caseload revenue, intake conversion, unsigned leads, after-hours missed calls, sign-up-to-settlement velocity. Never "our AI voice platform."

## 3. Call objectives — eliminating rejection
Every call has both:
- **Primary** — what you want them to *do* (book demo). Think big.
- **Secondary** — what you accomplish regardless (learn current vendor, plant seed for trigger event, secure permission to recontact, identify intake decision-maker).

> "Rejection is always your reaction to the response you receive."

If a secondary is always achieved, no call is a failure. Seed-planting line: *"Even though there's not a fit today, if you find that [trigger], keep in mind we can [result]."*

## 4. Voicemail strategy
Leave one; don't expect a callback — goal is curiosity ("I wonder what that is?"). Structure = opener formula + "I'll call you back [specific day]." Number spoken twice, slowly. Core message constant across attempts; only the Smart-intel hook varies.

**Banned on follow-up:** "Did you get the email I sent?" Do not hang up mid-message, do not impersonate familiarity, do not leave name+number only.

Last-resort message (after multiple attempts): *"I've tried several times to reach you about [PVP]. If I don't hear back, I'll assume this isn't something you'd like to discuss at this time."*

## 5. Gatekeepers — work *with*, not around
Treat screeners as you'd treat the buyer. They have info and influence.

**Don't:** refuse info, say "personal matter," ask "is he busy/in a meeting." **Do:** 4-step social engineering, learn their name, use it next time, ask buyer's preferences.

"Is this a sales call?" → *"I don't know yet. If there's a fit it might be; otherwise it's not. I work with [role] helping them [PVP], and I'd like to ask [name] a few questions to see if there's a basis for further conversation."*

"What is this about?" → short results-oriented opener, never the product name.

Go *high* when unsure — being referred down carries implicit clout.

## 6. Opening statements
Two and only two opener objectives: (1) move to receptive frame, (2) move to questioning. **Not** a pitch, appointment ask, or decision.

**4-step formula:**
1. Identify self + org.
2. Smart-intel sentence ("I was speaking with ___" / "I see your firm is now ___" / "My compliments on the article…").
3. Hint at PVP, weasel-worded.
4. Contingent question invitation: *"…I've got a few ideas that might be of value regarding ___, and I'd like to ask a few questions to see if it might be worth considering."*

**Hard banned phrases (each is a prompt rule):** "just calling," "wanted to introduce myself," "touching base," "reach out," "checking in," "did you get my email," "do you have a minute," "did I catch you at a bad time," "thanks for taking my call," "as you know," "I'm sure you'd agree," "if I could show you a way…," "are you the decision maker?," "are you the person in charge of ___?," "I'm not trying to sell you anything," "I'm calling people in your area," "I'm updating my database."

Time-respect, if used, is a **statement** embedded after PVP — never a question: *"…if I've reached you at a good time, I'd like to ask a few questions."*

## 7. Early resistance — verbatim templates
All responses = softener ("Oh," "I see," "I understand") + redirecting question. Never argue.

| Resistance | Response |
|---|---|
| "Not interested." | "I see. Where are you now getting your [X]?" — or — "Does that mean never, or just not now?" |
| "We're all set." | "I understand. When is your next project coming up?" |
| "We're happy with our supplier." | "That's fine. If something changed with that, would it be all right if I stay in touch? …What would need to change?" — or — "Teach me if you would — what kind of service would it take for you to feel *better* than satisfied?" |
| "Send literature." | "Happy to. So I can tailor it, I'd like to ask a few questions." |
| "Why should I consider you?" | "There might be a few reasons — I'll need to find out more about your situation first. I'd like to ask a few questions." (Never list reasons.) |
| "You've got two minutes." | "I'll happily call back when you have more time — there are details I need to learn before I can determine how much we could save you." |
| "What does it cost?" (early) | "It depends on several variables. Let me ask a few questions so I can quote the best price for your situation." |
| Confusing / contradictory | Play dumb. "I'm not following — could you explain?" |

Last-resort: *"Could you ever see situations where this would even be a possibility for you? What would those situations be?"*

## 8. Smart questions
**Avoid:** "Are you satisfied?" / "Any needs?" / "Any problems?" / "Did you know we…?" / "Of course you'd want to save money, right?"

**Assumptive Problem Question** — don't ask *if* the problem exists, assume it: *"What happens when ___?" / "How do you handle it when ___?" / "Tell me about situations where ___." / "What are the implications of ___?"*

**Loaded-benefit (third-party):** *"Most of our customers found they were having [pain]. What's your experience?"*

**Iceberg rule** — always a next question: "Tell me more." / "Please go on." / "Elaborate." / "Oh?"

**Quantify everything** — lost revenue, hours, missed calls per week. Needed for ROI framing.

**Decision process (depersonalize):** "Who aside from yourself would be involved?" / "What route does this take to get approved?" — never "Are you the decision maker?"

**Criteria elicitation (when they cite a current supplier):** *"What were the criteria you used when you chose your present supplier?"* — not "What do you like best about them?"

**Clarify fuzzy deferrals:** "Let's stay in touch" → "Great — so you plan to move forward eventually? When?"

## 9. Listening
- Top 10% reps talked ~12 min; bottom 10% ~30 min. Less is more.
- **Two mandatory pauses** (2–3s): after your question; after their answer. The second pause regularly extracts unsolicited info.
- Verbal nods: "Uh-huh," "I see," "Go on," "Tell me more."
- Lowered voice = attention — note what's said softly, it matters.
- **Mirror their exact words** in the recommendation. "They will not disagree with what they said."
- After stating a price, **stop talking**. No preemptive discount.

## 10. Recommendation & commitment
Use "recommendation," not pitch/presentation.

Sequence:
1. Transition: *"Based on what you told me about ___, I believe I have something here that will ___."*
2. Paraphrase their needs in their words; confirm.
3. Recommend **results**, not features. After each benefit, trial-close: *"Would that work for you?"*
4. Ask for action, not permission.

**Ask-for-action phrasings** (Gordon restaurant effect — no-shows dropped 30%→10% by changing "please call if you change your plans" to "*Will you* call us…?"):
- Not "May I send info?" → *"If I send you information, will you look it over and we can talk again?"*
- Not "May I call in a few weeks?" → *"Will we be able to talk more when I call back?"*
- "Are you personally sold on this? If it were your decision, are we the ones you would choose? Will you recommend our proposal?"
- *"What's the worst that could happen if you did this?"* / *"If you did nothing, where would you be?"*
- **"Own"** beats "buy." Ask for more than you expect (theory of contrast).

## 11. Wrapping up
Success of a follow-up is determined on the *previous* call. End every non-closing call with:
1. Why a follow-up is necessary (explicit).
2. What *you* will do.
3. What *they* will do — an assignment. No assignment, not a real prospect.
4. Specific time: *"Put me down for 11:15 your time."* Not "a couple weeks."
5. Summarize agreed needs + commitments before hanging up.

Post-call notes template — **ACTION**: Authority / Commitment made / Timing / Interest+Need / Overview delivered / Notes.

Self-review: only *"What did I like?"* and *"What would I have done differently?"* — never "what didn't I like."

## 12. Sounding smart
- Talk like yourself on the phone with a friend — no telemarketer cadence.
- Eliminate *uh, um, you know, like* (amplified by absence of visual channel).
- Verbal nods substitute for eye contact.
- Record + review every call.

## 13. Mindset (load-bearing)
- **Quality, not numbers.** A prior "no" has no bearing on the next call.
- **Call high.** Start above decision level; being referred down carries clout.
- Think big on primary objectives — if anyone has ever closed on a prospecting call, that's your ceiling.
- *"So what?"* reframe on any fear.

## 14. Case-study end-to-end template (Ch. 20)
The cleanest ready-to-steal pattern:

**Opener:**
> "[Name], this is [rep] from [co]. I've read your [public doc], and [screener] told me you're [specific situation]. What I do is help [role] get rid of [pain] and replace it with [benefit] — I can guarantee [specific outcome]. I'd like to ask some questions to see if I might provide you with some information."

**Three-bucket priority elicitation:**
> "When it comes to [category], I find [role]s normally care about 3 areas: [A], [B], [C]. Would you say that's accurate? Of those three, which matters most to you?"

**Conditional commitment ask:**
> "It seems we might have something worth a look. If there were a way for you to see options that help with [A], [B], and [C], as hassle-free as possible — would you be open to a conversation about some ideas?"

## Direct translation into the autocaller

**Prompt — ban-list:** *just calling, touch base, reach out, check in, did you get my email, do you have a minute, did I catch you at a bad time, I'm not trying to sell you anything, if I could show you a way, thanks for taking my call, as you know, I know you're busy, are you the decision maker, are you familiar with us.*

**Prompt — must-haves:** opener contains one tailored intel element; PVP names a **revenue/caseload outcome**; weasel words on all first-call value claims; time-respect is a statement after PVP, never a question up-front; mandatory 2–3s pause after question and after answer.

**Objection-handler state machine:** table in §7 drops in as verbatim response bank — each branch = softener + redirecting question, never a counter-argument.

**Lead model:** every lead needs `primary_objective` *and* `secondary_objective`. Judge scores whichever was accomplished → "no demo" with secondary achieved = partial win, not rejection.

**Judge rubric additions:**
- Opener contained a specific Smart-intel signal?
- PVP named an outcome, not a product?
- Weasel words used before pain was confirmed?
- Pauses after questions/answers?
- Resistance met with softener + redirecting question (not argument)?
- At least one assumptive-problem or loaded-benefit question before recommending?
- Mirrored at least one prospect phrase in the recommendation?
- Call ended with explicit "what I'll do / what you'll do / when we'll talk next"?
- Avoided pricing, features, and appointment ask in first 15 seconds?
- Secondary objective attempted and recorded?

**Voicemail CLI template:** opener-formula body + "I'll call you back [specific day] — my number is X, I'll repeat that, X." Constant core; only Smart-intel hook rotates. After N attempts, permit the last-resort message.

**Self-review prompt (for reflection loops):** only "what did I like / what would I do differently" — never "what didn't I like."
